Land mutation is processed in Bangladesh as per the Land Management Manual, 1990 and is performed by the Assistant Commissioner (Land) at the Upazila Land Office. **The Core Document Checklist to Train On:** The mutation application is submitted at the relevant Upazila or district land office; the application must include Form 6 (mutation application form), supporting documents (sale deed, khatian, tax receipts, NID), and a power of attorney if represented by a lawyer. **The Khatian System (Critical Conceptual Knowledge):** There are different types of khatians: CS Khatian (Cadastral Survey) — the earliest historical record; SA Khatian (State Acquisition Survey) — updated after state acquisition acts; RS Khatian (Revisional Survey) — the latest corrected record; and BS Khatian (Bangladesh Survey) — in areas where the BS survey is complete. **Online Portals:** For online checks in 2025, you can use: `www.land.gov.bd` for mutation, khatian, and application status; `www.minland.gov.bd` for policy and digital services; and `dlrs.gov.bd` (DLRS Portal) for map and survey records. However, these are still partial databases, so always cross-check physically at the relevant land office. **Critical Warning to Train Into the LLM:** A Khatian does not create or abolish titles — it is simply a record of physical possession at the time of preparation. ⚙️ ONE FINAL STRUCTURAL NOTE The single most important architectural decision for training is this: for every domain, you need **three layers of content**, not just one: 1. **The Law / The Form** — The raw official text or PDF 2. **The Procedure** — Step-by-step process in plain Bengali (many of which already exist as NGO guides from BLAST, ASK, etc.) 3. **The Lived Experience** — Actual pain points, common errors, fraud patterns, things that "should work but don't" — this is the hardest to get and the most valuable For Layer 3, your best sources will be: legal aid NGO case records, court filing services, Reddit-equivalent Bangladeshi forums (Bissoy, Quora Bengali), and YouTube tutorial comments. That is where the real confusion lives — and your LLM must be trained on the *confusion*, not just the *rules*.
