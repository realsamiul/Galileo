# Master Infrastructure Map of Primary Data Sources for Bengali Civic and Legal Large Language Models
## SECTION 1 — VERIFIED SOURCE REGISTRY
Developing a localized, high-fidelity Bengali Large Language Model (LLM) designed to democratize access to administrative, civic, and legal workflows in Bangladesh requires a comprehensive audit of the primary digital data infrastructure. This registry evaluates each identified data repository across twelve specific operational and technical criteria. The sources are classified into logical categories to establish a clear pipeline for data collection and model training.
### A. Government Administrative Portals
#### 1. Bangladesh National Portal (bangladesh.gov.bd)
 * **Current URL Accessibility:** Active and accessible.
 * **Access Barriers:** Open access; no registration or payment wall.
 * **Content Types:** Directory structures of over 53,000 government offices, citizen charters, local notices, circulars, and departmental links.
 * **Language Composition:** Mixed; primarily Bengali, with secondary English translation toggles for major divisions.
 * **Document Formats:** HTML, PDF, and scanned image PDFs.
 * **Machine Readability:** High for structural HTML pages; low for localized administrative notices and citizens' charters, which are frequently uploaded as scanned images.
 * **Estimated Volume:** Millions of dynamically linked pages across thousands of nested institutional sub-portals.
 * **Update Frequency and Maintenance:** Actively maintained with daily administrative updates.
 * **Extraction Methods (Bulk vs. Single):** Single-page browsing; bulk collection requires distributed web crawlers.
 * **API or Structured Endpoints:** No public API or structured data export options.
 * **LLM Training Value:** HIGH — Serves as the definitive directory for mapping government institutions, understanding department roles, and learning official Bengali administrative terminology.
 * **Expected vs. Actual Gaps:** Frequent broken links to local government subdomains, and many local notices are uploaded as low-resolution, non-searchable image files.
#### 2. Government Forms Portal (forms.portal.gov.bd)
 * **Current URL Accessibility:** Active and accessible.
 * **Access Barriers:** Open access; no registration or payment wall.
 * **Content Types:** Official administrative forms, including passport applications, pension forms, driving licenses, and land mutation requests.
 * **Language Composition:** Primarily Bengali, with occasional bilingual fields.
 * **Document Formats:** PDF, DOC, and DOCX.
 * **Machine Readability:** Mixed; standard federal forms are fillable PDFs, but older localized forms are scanned, image-only documents.
 * **Estimated Volume:** Approximately 1,500 distinct administrative forms.
 * **Update Frequency and Maintenance:** Actively maintained.
 * **Extraction Methods (Bulk vs. Single):** Single-file downloads; bulk extraction requires a custom crawler.
 * **API or Structured Endpoints:** No public API.
 * **LLM Training Value:** HIGH — Essential for teaching the model the exact fields and structural layouts of civic documents, helping it assist citizens with automated form-filling.
 * **Expected vs. Actual Gaps:** Standardized schema definitions (such as JSON Schema or XML templates) are missing, requiring manual parsing of PDF layouts.

## SECTION 4 — CRITICAL GAPS
This research has identified several significant gaps in the public availability of digital civic and legal data in Bangladesh. These gaps represent major obstacles to training a high-fidelity model on public data alone.
### Legal Decisions and Case Law Reports
The digitization of Bangladeshi case law remains highly fragmented and largely locked behind private commercial walls:
 * *Dhaka Law Reports (DLR):* The oldest and most authoritative legal journal in Bangladesh (established in 1949) is a private commercial venture. It is not officially digitized for free public use, and the publisher's business model relies on physical, paper-bound print editions. No public, open-access text database of DLR exists.
 * *Bangladesh Legal Decisions (BLD):* The official legal reporting platform of the Bangladesh Bar Council has been digitized through its proprietary platform (bldlegalized.com). It contains over 40 years of Supreme Court judgments, summaries, and headnotes. However, this data is gated behind a payment subscription wall and is protected against automated data extraction.
 * *Public Court Judgments:* There is no comprehensive, centralized, and free public database of lower court or district court judgments. The official Supreme Court website offers a basic case search tool, but its historical records are incomplete, and it lacks the advanced search features needed for deep legal research.
### Statutory Land Management Manuals
 * *Land Management Manual 1990:* This foundational guide outlines 15 key registers and 3 annual returns used by Union, Upazila, and District authorities to manage land records and revenue. However, it is not available as a comprehensive, structured digital publication from the government. Instead, only partial summaries, outlines, and analytical extracts of these 15 registers are available through third-party platforms (such as Scribd) and academic law journals.
```
  [Physical Registers] ----(Scanned/Incomplete)----> [Scribd / Law Journals]
  (Union/Upazila Offices)                             (Fragmented Legal Summaries)

```
The absence of an official, machine-readable digital edition of the 1990 Manual makes it difficult to train an LLM on the precise rules of land administration.
### Local Government Infrastructure and Forms
 * *Union Parishad & City Corporation Forms:* There is no centralized database for localized administrative forms. While standard forms (such as birth registration applications) are hosted on the national forms portal, many specific local government templates remain offline. These forms must be physically collected from individual Union Parishad, Upazila, or City Corporation offices, as they are not digitized or centralized anywhere online.
### National Open Data Frameworks
 * While some countries maintain active, central open data initiatives (such as data.gov in the US), Bangladesh's equivalent portal (data.gov.bd) has stalled. Launched in 2016 by the Prime Minister's Office and a2i, the portal was designed to host open datasets from over 35 ministries. However, many of these datasets are static and have not been updated since their initial release, leaving developers without a reliable source of fresh civic or administrative data.
